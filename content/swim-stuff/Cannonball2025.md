## Cannonball 2025, Gurgaon

First pool race. First 10k race. Some nerves and jitters at the beginning of the race, forgot to start the swim session on Garmin, started to record only after 18 laps - 1800 meters.

It became clear very quickly that I am not competing with the other swimmers in the lane! Having never done a 10k before, I was worried that if I push too hard, I may not be able to complete. The longest I swam for practice was 5.8k! In hindsight, I could have pushed a bit harder and shaved 15-20 minutes off the time.

Most satisfying thing I heard during the race: “last lap”

My coach (Rachna) showed me a white-board, which I guess read, "Don't count every lap”. I have seen coaches communicate to swimmers this way on TV and enjoyed it thoroughly! I thought to myself- Am I a swimmer now?! 

My neck started to hurt while rotating to breathe! On the pain scale, it may be a 4/10. But every time I turned my head, I felt a sprain. 


![Start of the race](/images/swim/cb2.jpeg)

Mid-race thoughts: what am I doing here! Do I even belong here?

Learnt a lot of important lessons - Work on technique - catch, pull, glide, kick, EVF - all need to improve. I also realised it's only a 10k and I could put in more effort. I don't need to think about preserving energy as if its a channel swim. 


![Finishing touch](/images/swim/cb3.jpeg)

Thinking back, I also thought I don't just walk past the finish line like it is a formality! Take a moment to enjoy the finish line.
